About PVAZ

Photovoltaic (PV) modules, commonly known as solar panels, can be found around the globe and in a variety of environments. To help comparisons of PV module performance, standard test conditions (STC) were conceived in labs and measured to assure a consistent spectrum of analysis. These particular test conditions do not always line up with the real world, and as such a different standard was derived, called PVUSA test condition. This latter standard took place in California, and, while more attuned to real world conditions than STC, still lacked performance prediction in more extreme environments, such as deserts. This project, dubbed PVAZ, attempts to predict PV performance using weather and PV module data originating from Mesa, Arizona.
